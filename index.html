CREATORS not CONSUMERS

Almost anything exciting and important in the world will be based on computers. The language of computers "software" is commonly known as 'code'. Learning to code or at least understanding the fundementals of how it works has become a key skill, just like english and mathematics. Coding is almost a super human power which enables us to share our intellectual powers at scales never seen before

We at Aussie Kids Code can help your kids understand how computer programmes are made using project centric learning methods that are more engaging and fun. During the process kids will learn vital skills like logical thinking, problem solving and team work. They will start appreciating how day to day items that we take for granted are made and the human effort and thought processes behind them. Instead of teaching them to "CONSUME" we teach them to "CREATE".

Offerings

Inspire

Kids as young as 5 years of age are found to be quite capable of understading the underlying logic of a computer programme. We use visual teaching tools based on popular games like angry birds to teach little kids, the basics of computational thinking. We are also big believers in the effects of the wow factor.

Upskill

Young kids in primary school who can read well or early stages of high school, can be gradually exposed to real world programming languages and techniques. We pay more attention to tailoring these sessions based on individual kids needs, depending on the interest and previous exposure of each kid.

Masters

This programme is designed for young kids who already has a passion for computers and coding and want to expand their horizons in the ever changing world of computer programming. We will start working on their existing skills and gradually inject with critical knowledge shaping them in to real programming super stars.

Who are we?.

We are a group of industry professionals with a background on Computer Engineering. We work full time on large scale software systems development here in Perth. We are all passionate about technology and dissapointed at the same time about the lack of enthusiasm in the Australian community towards computer science and programming. That's why we are trying to do something about it.

Most importantly, we are loving parents to small kids. Kids who are excited with no reservations when it comes to learning new experiences and expressing themselves in novel ways.
