Teaching kids to code

Almost anything exciting and important in the world now, and in the future will be either based or supported by software programmes. The language of software programmes is commonly known as 'code'. Learning to code or at least understanding how it works will be a key skill in the near future, just like mathematics and language.

Thousands of the most highly paid and rewarding jobs of the future may go unfulfilled, because coding is not taught at Australian schools yet. Countries like USA and UK has already introduced coding at primary schools. Even developing countries like Vietnam are taking software very seriously and altered their curriculums to include it. Australian government has just started noticing this. It has passed a 1 billion dollar package under innovation which aims to cover a lot of ground in software development both at industry level and at our schools. Unlike many things in politics the importance of introducing coding in to school curriculum has gained bi-partian approval.

Aussie Kids Code can help your kids understand how computer programmes are made. Instead of teaching them to "CONSUME" we teach them to "CREATE".
